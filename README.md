# Priority-Based Mindset Mapping

```text
                         WHAT RULES?

          VALUE             STABILITY          RESOURCES
         Dignity            Security          Acquisition
            ↕                  ↕                   ↕
       Recognition          Harmony             Comfort
```

The arrows mark paired directions within each domain, not zero-sum sliders. Both sides can be strong; the question is how much weight each carries and which one tends to prevail when they conflict.

> **A mindset is a geometry of priorities with direction and magnitude.**

A person does not merely possess priorities. They are organized by them.

Two people may admire the same virtues, repeat the same beliefs, and still move through the world in opposite ways. One protects dignity at the cost of harmony. Another preserves harmony until dignity becomes expendable. One hunts opportunity through discomfort. Another abandons opportunity when comfort is disturbed.

Pressure reveals the hidden order.

Priority-Based Mindset Mapping begins with three domains of human life—**Value, Stability, and Resources**—each divided into two continuous directions. Their relative intensity, governing order, and interaction form a changing landscape of attention and action.

Recurring shapes in that landscape become archetypal masks: King, Fool, Steward, Trickster, and others. The names are not permanent personality types. They are landmarks that compress coherent regions of the priority field into forms a human being can recognize and remember.

Context changes the weights. The same person may repeatedly occupy one mask in creative work, another with money, another in friendship, and another under threat. Averaging those situations into a single score can erase the very structure the map is meant to reveal.

The useful question is not which mask a person *is* in the abstract, but which masks recur in which situations—and whether awareness of those patterns allows greater freedom in how they show up.

```text
RECURRING MASK  →  RIGID PATTERN
                         │
              if it crowds out the field
                         ↓
                      CAPTURE
```

A strong priority is not a sickness. Capture begins when one configuration becomes compulsory across situations, rejects correction, and flattens the rest of the priority field.
