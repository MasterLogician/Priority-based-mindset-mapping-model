# Priority-Based Mindset Mapping

**Priority-Based Mindset Mapping (PBMM)** is a framework for understanding how people, groups, and institutions organize perception and action around competing priorities.

Its central premise is:

> **A mindset is a geometry of priorities with direction and magnitude.**

People may care about many of the same things yet behave very differently because those things are weighted, ordered, and combined differently.

PBMM compresses this motivational field into three domains:

| Domain | Human layer | Establishing direction | Realizing direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Value** | Spirit | **Dignity** | **Recognition** |
| **Stability** | Nervous system | **Security** | **Harmony** |
| **Resources** | Body | **Acquisition** | **Comfort** |

The establishing directions have been provisionally described as masculine: they establish, separate, defend, extend, or obtain. The realizing directions have been provisionally described as feminine: they receive, relate, integrate, cultivate, reflect, or convert potential into lived experience. These are symbolic directions present in every person, not rigid classifications of men and women.

## The basic idea

Every person contains all six priorities.

A person may have strong dignity, moderate recognition, high acquisition, low comfort, moderate security, and high harmony. The relationship among those weights forms a priority configuration.

PBMM is interested especially in:

- what a person notices first;
- what they instinctively protect;
- what they pursue without prompting;
- what loss most destabilizes them;
- and what they sacrifice when priorities conflict.

Tradeoffs often reveal priority order more clearly than stated beliefs.

## Archetypes as compressed configurations

Recurring priority configurations can be represented by archetypal masks.

A mask is not a permanent personality type. It is a culturally compressed attentional and behavioral program: a recognizable way of organizing the world around a governing priority and a supporting priority.

For example:

- **Dignity governing Recognition** produces the **King** region.
- **Recognition governing Dignity** produces the **Queen** region.
- **Acquisition governing Recognition** produces the **Fool** region.
- **Recognition governing Acquisition** produces the **Trickster** region.

The full system contains nine cross-polar pairings and eighteen directions of governance. Some names remain provisional; the structural pairing matters more than the current label.

## Integration

PBMM is not mainly a typing system. Its deeper concern is integration.

A healthy individual can access multiple priority functions and move between masks as reality requires. Pathology begins when one priority or archetypal function attempts to become the whole personality, rejects correction, and subordinates every other function.

The same pattern may scale socially:

> **The unintegrated psyche becomes psychologically captured. The unintegrated society externalizes its partial archetypes as ideological factions struggling for total rule.**

A function becomes pathological when it ceases serving the whole and attempts to become the whole.

## How to use PBMM

PBMM analysis should remain probabilistic.

Do not begin by declaring that someone *is* an archetype. Begin with uncertainty, observe repeated behavior and tradeoffs, update competing interpretations, and distinguish a temporary state from a durable attractor.

A useful analysis sequence is:

1. Identify the dominant domain: Value, Stability, or Resources.
2. Identify the governing direction within that domain.
3. Identify the strongest supporting priority from the opposite polarity.
4. Compare the resulting archetypal possibilities.
5. Seek evidence that would disconfirm the current interpretation.
6. Preserve secondary masks and contextual variation.

## Epistemic status

PBMM is an interpretive and developmental framework, not a validated psychological assessment, clinical diagnosis, or completed scientific theory.

Its current claims are provisional. The three domains, six priorities, polar language, vector representation, and archetypal names should be retained only insofar as they preserve useful information, support reliable interpretation, and survive criticism.

The model should ultimately be judged by whether it helps people:

- perceive motivational patterns more accurately;
- understand disagreement without flattening it into moral caricature;
- identify psychological and institutional imbalance;
- expand their behavioral repertoire;
- and integrate competing human functions into a more coherent whole.
