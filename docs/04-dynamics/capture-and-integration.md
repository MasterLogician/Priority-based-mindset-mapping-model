# Capture and Integration

Reading priorities tells us which configurations recur. Integration asks whether those coherent regions can remain responsive to context—or whether one pattern becomes compulsory.

A strong priority is not a sickness. A familiar mask is not a prison.

```text
RECURRING MASK  →  RIGIDITY
                      │
           if it crowds out the field
                      ↓
                   CAPTURE
```

The names remain under construction; the priority shapes are the point. Every archetype carries a partial truth. The King sees dignity and rightful order. The Paladin sees danger, duty, and the cost of weak boundaries. The Great Mother sees vulnerability and the need for belonging. The Fool sees openings that caution would miss. The Steward remembers what must be preserved.

The pattern becomes pathological when it claims the whole field.

> **Capture begins when one coherent region becomes compulsory across situations.**

## Capture

A captured mask reads nearly every situation through the same priority shape. It crowds out competing signals, resists correction, and turns its characteristic threat into a permanent emergency.

The captured King experiences correction as humiliation. The captured Paladin experiences ambiguity as betrayal. The captured Great Mother experiences separation as harm. The captured Fool experiences limits as imprisonment and completion as the death of possibility. The captured Steward experiences experiment as irresponsible loss.

None of these perceptions is invented from nothing. Each begins with something real. Pathology lies in the loss of proportion and contextual movement.

Capture also produces its own evidence. Excessive Security suppresses disagreement, hides information, and creates the insecurity used to justify more control. Recognition dependence weakens the dignity it tries to prove, increasing the hunger for another witness. Endless Acquisition prevents restoration, creates depletion, and makes further Acquisition feel urgent.

The loop wounds the system, then presents the wound as proof that the loop must continue.

## Contextual masks and fragmentation

Different masks in different parts of life are not automatically fragmentation.

A person may be a Fool in creation, a Steward with money, a Gentleman in friendship, and a Paladin when protecting family. These can be coherent regions of identity shaped by different situations.

Fragmentation begins when those regions become sealed from one another: when lessons learned in one context cannot cross into another, when values appear and disappear without awareness, or when one mask treats every competing priority as alien or contemptible.

The inner Paladin condemns the Fool as reckless. The Fool mocks the Steward as dead. The Great Mother experiences dignity and separation as cruelty. The King experiences dependence and care as humiliation.

Each region builds a case against the others. The person changes context, and with the context changes memory, permission, posture, and judgment.

Integration does not erase these differences. It allows the regions to remain distinct without becoming mutually unintelligible.

## Awareness and intentionality

PBMM does not require a hidden executive standing above the masks and choosing among them.

Awareness begins more simply: noticing the active priority shape, articulating what is being protected or pursued, and observing how that shape alters perception and persona.

Meditation, reflection, and deliberate attention can create room around a recurring mask. Mapping the drive does not make it disappear, but it can make the pattern easier to metabolize. What was previously enacted without awareness becomes something the person can recognize while it is happening.

This does not imply perfect control or the ability to select any mask on command. It means greater intentionality in how one participates in the pattern and how one shows up in the world.

> **The mask becomes less absolute when its priority shape can be seen and named.**

Integration is not one permanent balance. It is the capacity for different coherent regions to coexist, inform one another, and remain responsive to the situation.

## Archetypal civil war

A similar pattern can appear in society.

A civilization needs differentiated priority logics. A military must weight Security differently from a hospice. A court organizes Dignity and Recognition differently from a workshop. A household, monastery, laboratory, market, and festival each hold a different part of human life.

Specialization is not the danger. A society becomes sick when one priority logic mistakes its local importance for universal moral law.

The Paladin warns that boundaries and duty cannot be wished away. The Great Mother warns that order without care becomes inhuman. The King carries legitimacy and standards. The Trickster exposes hypocrisy and dead form. The Architect turns intention into structure. The Empress asks whether material life has become fertile and inhabitable.

Civil war begins when each says:

> **I am not one necessary region among others. I am the moral truth of the whole.**

Then correction becomes attack. Boundaries become cruelty. Care becomes weakness. Dignity becomes domination. Criticism becomes treason. Preservation becomes cowardice. Experiment becomes desecration.

The factions no longer argue inside a shared order. They deny that the order is shared.

```text
PARTIAL TRUTH
     ↓
MORAL MONOPOLY
     ↓
CORRECTION READ AS ATTACK
     ↓
ARCHETYPAL CIVIL WAR
```

The resulting society may produce Security without legitimacy, Harmony enforced through silence, Recognition without truth, Comfort without production, Acquisition without inhabitable ends, or Dignity without reciprocity.

## Integration at scale

At social scale, PBMM describes institutional and cultural priority logics—not a civilization as though it possessed one literal psyche.

Integration is not bland moderation and not the equal weighting of every priority. It means each necessary region has a legitimate channel, each can correct and limit the others, none is permanently demonized, and none receives total rule.

Customs, rites, laws, disciplines, and inherited institutions can store this coordination across generations. A comfortable society may preserve dignity and acquisition through apprenticeship, service, physical discipline, and rites of responsibility. A harsh society may preserve harmony and comfort through hospitality, sanctuary, festivals, charity, and limits on vengeance.

Healthy custom trains what the immediate environment neglects.

The same diagnostic questions can be carried from a person into a family, institution, political coalition, or civilization:

```text
What is inflated?
What is neglected?
What has been demonized?
Which priority logic has become compulsory?
Which corrective signal is being misread as attack?
```

The unintegrated individual becomes trapped in rigid or sealed regions of identity. The unintegrated society gives those partial regions flags, institutions, and enemies.

> **Integration is not the absence of conflict. It is the capacity to keep conflict inside a shared map.**
