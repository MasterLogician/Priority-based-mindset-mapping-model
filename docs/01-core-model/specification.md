# Canonical PBMM Specification

**Status:** Working specification for PBMM v0.4

This document defines the current structural core of Priority-Based Mindset Mapping. Application essays may explore the model more freely, but they should not silently alter the definitions here.

## 1. Core claim

PBMM models a mindset as a changing configuration of weighted priorities.

> **A mindset is a geometry of priorities with direction and magnitude.**

The word *geometry* is currently conceptual rather than a claim that PBMM has already been established as a calibrated mathematical space.

## 2. Three domains

PBMM compresses motivation into three broad domains.

| Domain | Symbolic human layer | Fundamental process | Central question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Value** | Spirit | Meaning and worth | What possesses value, and what is my rightful relation to it? |
| **Stability** | Nervous system | Regulation and coherence | What keeps the system safe, coordinated, and intact? |
| **Resources** | Body | Supply and metabolism | What must be obtained, used, restored, or enjoyed? |

These correspondences are symbolic anchors, not literal anatomical compartments.

## 3. Six priorities

Each domain has two complementary directions.

| Domain | Establishing direction | Realizing direction |
|---|---|---|
| **Value** | **Dignity** | **Recognition** |
| **Stability** | **Security** | **Harmony** |
| **Resources** | **Acquisition** | **Comfort** |

### Dignity

Establishes worth through self-respect, integrity, sovereignty, honor, rightful standing, and resistance to degradation.

### Recognition

Realizes worth through acknowledgment, visibility, representation, belonging, reputation, and accurate social reflection.

### Security

Establishes stability through boundaries, protection, preparation, vigilance, deterrence, redundancy, and control of threats.

### Harmony

Realizes stability through trust, cooperation, coordination, reconciliation, co-regulation, shared norms, and reduced relational friction.

### Acquisition

Establishes resources by obtaining or creating food, tools, money, territory, information, skills, capability, energy, and opportunity.

### Comfort

Realizes resources as nourishment, restoration, pleasure, shelter, beauty, familiarity, convenience, and inhabitable wellbeing.

## 4. Polar language

PBMM provisionally uses **masculine** for the establishing directions and **feminine** for the realizing directions.

The masculine direction tends to establish, separate, defend, extend, impose form, or obtain.

The feminine direction tends to receive, relate, integrate, cultivate, reflect, contain, or turn potential into lived experience.

This language is symbolic and descriptive. It does not imply that men possess only one direction or women only the other. Every person and functioning social system requires both.

The language remains open to revision if a superior compression preserves the structure with less distortion.

## 5. Priority configuration

A person's configuration may be represented conceptually as:

\[
\mathbf{p}=(D,R,S,H,A,C)
\]

where each component represents the relative weight of a priority.

PBMM does not currently prescribe a validated numerical scale. Weights may initially be estimated qualitatively or probabilistically.

## 6. Direction and intensity

Within each domain, two distinct properties matter.

### Polar direction

\[
\mathbf{x}=(D-R,\;S-H,\;A-C)
\]

This represents which direction governs within Value, Stability, and Resources.

### Domain intensity

\[
\mathbf{i}=(D+R,\;S+H,\;A+C)
\]

This prevents an important loss of information. A person high in both dignity and recognition should not be treated as equivalent to a person low in both merely because their difference is similar.

The compact model therefore requires both **direction** and **intensity**.

## 7. Governance

An archetypal pairing contains:

1. a **governing priority**, which determines the central aim and interpretation;
2. a **supporting priority**, which supplies the characteristic strategy.

Order matters.

- Dignity governing Recognition is structurally different from Recognition governing Dignity.
- Acquisition governing Harmony is structurally different from Harmony governing Acquisition.

The same two priorities can therefore produce two related but distinct masks.

## 8. The eighteen archetypal directions

The system pairs each establishing priority with each realizing priority, producing nine pairings. Reversing governance produces eighteen directions.

Archetypes are:

- culturally compressed names for recurring configurations;
- attentional and behavioral programs;
- regions or attractors in priority space;
- masks that may be entered, exited, integrated, or become rigid.

They are **not** permanent identities or clinical categories.

The structural pairings remain more authoritative than the current names. Several names are explicitly provisional.

## 9. State, trait, and attractor

PBMM distinguishes:

- **State:** a temporary configuration produced by immediate conditions;
- **habitual configuration:** a recurrent pattern across situations;
- **attractor:** a configuration toward which the system repeatedly returns after disturbance.

An observer should not infer an enduring archetype from a single episode.

## 10. Bayesian interpretation

The priority vector is latent: it cannot be observed directly.

Analysis should therefore proceed through probabilistic updating.

1. Begin with multiple plausible configurations.
2. Observe what the person notices, protects, pursues, fears losing, and sacrifices.
3. Update the relative plausibility of each interpretation.
4. Give special weight to repeated tradeoffs.
5. Seek disconfirming evidence.
6. Preserve uncertainty and alternative explanations.

The correct output is not "this person is a King," but something closer to:

> Given the available evidence, Dignity governing Recognition is currently the strongest interpretation, with Security governing Recognition as a meaningful alternative.

## 11. Integration and pathology

A strong priority is not automatically pathological.

Health depends on whether priorities remain:

- proportionate;
- reality-responsive;
- open to correction;
- coordinated with the rest of the system;
- and available without becoming compulsory.

A priority becomes pathological when it ceases serving the whole and begins recruiting the whole system into serving itself.

This may occur through:

- excess or hypertrophy;
- deficiency or neglect;
- misordering of means and ends;
- fear-driven capture;
- or a self-sealing feedback loop.

## 12. Development

The developmental goal is not equal balance at every moment.

It is increased:

- repertoire;
- integration;
- proportionality;
- conscious selection;
- and reversibility.

A mature person can enter the mask appropriate to the task without confusing that mask with the total self.

## 13. Scale

PBMM may be applied at several levels:

- individual;
- relationship;
- family;
- team;
- institution;
- political coalition;
- civilization;
- engineered decision system.

At larger scales, the model should describe a distribution or aggregate pattern rather than pretending that a group is a single person.

## 14. Non-claims

PBMM does not currently claim:

- that the six priorities are exhaustive beyond revision;
- that its vector space has been empirically calibrated;
- that public behavior reveals private identity with certainty;
- that archetypes are fixed biological kinds;
- that symbolic masculine and feminine directions map perfectly onto sex;
- that the system is a diagnostic instrument;
- or that synthetic examples validate the theory.

## 15. Revision rule

A concept should remain foundational only while it improves compression, discrimination, explanation, prediction, or deliberate action.

The theory should change when a competing structure:

- explains more with less distortion;
- produces better observer agreement;
- predicts tradeoffs more accurately;
- or integrates anomalous cases more coherently.
