# Vector-Space Interpretation

PBMM can be represented conceptually as a six-dimensional priority space.

This formalism is intended to clarify the structure. It should not be mistaken for an empirically calibrated psychological measurement system.

## Six-dimensional configuration

Let:

\[
\mathbf{p}=(D,R,S,H,A,C)
\]

where:

- \(D\) = Dignity
- \(R\) = Recognition
- \(S\) = Security
- \(H\) = Harmony
- \(A\) = Acquisition
- \(C\) = Comfort

Each value represents the relative weight or salience of a priority in a person, group, or system.

## Domain direction

The governing direction within each domain can be represented as:

\[
\mathbf{x}=(D-R,\;S-H,\;A-C)
\]

The three coordinates describe:

- Value: Dignity ↔ Recognition
- Stability: Security ↔ Harmony
- Resources: Acquisition ↔ Comfort

The sign indicates direction. The absolute magnitude indicates how strongly one pole governs the other.

## Domain intensity

Subtraction alone loses information. A system high in both Dignity and Recognition could have the same difference as one low in both.

Domain intensity can therefore be represented as:

\[
\mathbf{i}=(D+R,\;S+H,\;A+C)
\]

A useful compact description requires both:

- **direction** — which pole governs;
- **intensity** — how strongly the entire domain is activated.

## Archetypes as regions

The eighteen archetypes can be understood as named regions or recurring attractors in the six-dimensional space.

For example:

- **King:** Dignity and Recognition are highly salient, with Dignity governing.
- **Queen:** the same pair is salient, with Recognition governing.
- **Fool:** Acquisition and Recognition are salient, with Acquisition governing.
- **Trickster:** the same pair is salient, with Recognition governing.

The remaining four priorities shape subtype, maturity, contradiction, and expression.

A person is therefore not reducible to one archetype. The archetype names a recognizable local structure within a larger configuration.

## Time and movement

The configuration changes through time:

\[
\mathbf{p}_{t+1}=F(\mathbf{p}_t,\;e_t,\;b_t,\;f_t)
\]

where the next state depends conceptually on:

- current priority weights;
- environmental conditions;
- bodily and nervous-system state;
- behavior;
- and social feedback.

This makes PBMM closer to a dynamical field than a static personality inventory.

## States and attractors

A temporary threat may sharply increase Security. Public performance may increase Recognition. Fatigue may elevate Comfort. Opportunity may elevate Acquisition.

These are states.

An attractor is a configuration toward which the system repeatedly returns. For example, a person may repeatedly interpret uncertainty through a Guardian-like security configuration or opportunity through a Fool-like acquisition configuration.

The strength of an attractor can be estimated by asking:

- How often does the pattern recur?
- How many contexts activate it?
- How quickly does the person return to it after disruption?
- How costly is it to leave?
- Does disconfirming evidence alter it?

## Corruption as capture

A priority becomes pathological not merely because its magnitude is high, but because it captures the system.

A local feedback loop begins to subordinate every other function.

Examples:

### Value capture

\[
D \rightarrow R \rightarrow D
\]

Fragile Dignity demands Recognition. Recognition temporarily reinforces the self-image, making future failures of Recognition more threatening.

### Stability capture

\[
S \rightarrow H \rightarrow S
\]

Security demands conformity in the name of Harmony. Suppressed disagreement conceals information, which produces greater insecurity and further control.

### Resource capture

\[
A \rightarrow C \rightarrow A
\]

Acquisition feeds appetite. Comfort ceases restoring the system and instead generates demand for more Acquisition.

The structural principle is:

> **A local priority loop becomes pathological when it recruits the global system into serving itself.**

## Development as controllability

Development can be understood as increasing control over movement through the space.

A mature system has greater:

- range;
- flexibility;
- proportionality;
- access to neglected priorities;
- ability to interrupt captured loops;
- and ability to return from a mask once its task is complete.

The goal is not a permanently centered vector. Different realities require different configurations.

The goal is that the configuration remains responsive to reality rather than compulsively returning to one narrow attractor.

## Bayesian estimation

The vector is latent and must be inferred from evidence.

An observer begins with uncertainty over possible configurations, then updates based on repeated behavior.

Tradeoffs are especially diagnostic because they reveal governance:

- Does the person sacrifice Harmony to preserve Dignity?
- Do they sacrifice Comfort to pursue Acquisition?
- Do they surrender Recognition to protect Security?

A rigorous PBMM analysis should produce a probability distribution or ranked set of interpretations, not a categorical verdict.

## Aggregate spaces

For a group, institution, or civilization, a single vector is an aggressive compression.

A better representation would include:

- the distribution of individual configurations;
- institutional weighting;
- role specialization;
- dominant public narratives;
- and the feedback structure determining which priorities are rewarded.

An institution may amplify Security even when its members are individually diverse. A culture may reward Recognition while materially depending on Acquisition. Aggregate analysis must preserve these distinctions.

## Epistemic boundary

The vector representation currently provides:

- conceptual clarity;
- explicit separation of direction and intensity;
- a language for movement and attractors;
- and a bridge to Bayesian and dynamical analysis.

It does not yet provide:

- validated units;
- proven orthogonal dimensions;
- reliable numerical scoring;
- or evidence that human motivation is literally linear.

Those questions belong to the research program rather than the settled core.
