# The Archetype System

PBMM generates archetypes by pairing one establishing priority with one realizing priority.

There are three establishing priorities:

- Dignity
- Security
- Acquisition

There are three realizing priorities:

- Recognition
- Harmony
- Comfort

This produces nine pairings. Each pairing can be governed in either direction, producing eighteen archetypal masks.

## Governance matters

The same two priorities form different masks depending on which one governs.

- Dignity governing Recognition asks how acknowledgment can serve rightful worth.
- Recognition governing Dignity asks how embodied worth can secure acknowledged position.

The pair supplies the structure. Governance supplies direction.

## Why the names matter

An archetypal name should do more than describe a job.

It should activate a symbolic library containing:

- characteristic aims;
- bodily attitude;
- social role;
- virtue;
- shadow;
- mythic precedent;
- and a recognizable mode of attention.

The name should compress rather than merely label.

That is why **King**, **Queen**, **Fool**, and **Trickster** carry more information than abstract labels such as “status-oriented type” or “novelty-recognition configuration.”

## Archetypes as masks

A mask is a coherent attentional program.

Entering a mask changes:

- what becomes salient;
- what feels threatened;
- what evidence is noticed;
- which actions feel natural;
- how the body prepares;
- and which internal voice interprets events.

For example:

- The King notices legitimacy, rank, insult, responsibility, and whether authority is acknowledged.
- The Paladin notices danger to the order, broken oaths, weak boundaries, and people requiring protection.
- The Fool notices openings, novelty, unexplored territory, and the chance to become visible through discovery.
- The Great Mother notices vulnerability, exclusion, unmet needs, and whether the group feels safely contained.

## Archetypes are not identities

A person can occupy several regions of the priority space.

Someone may:

- use the Gentleman mask in relationships;
- the Steward mask with money;
- the Fool mask in creative exploration;
- and the Paladin mask when protecting family.

PBMM should therefore describe:

- a primary attractor;
- secondary masks;
- neglected functions;
- and transitions between configurations.

The statement “this person is a King” is less accurate than:

> This person repeatedly returns to a Dignity-governing-Recognition configuration, especially under questions of status, responsibility, and public legitimacy.

## Virtue and shadow

Every archetype contains a functional truth and a characteristic danger.

The shadow is not an unrelated defect. It often grows from the same priority structure that produces the virtue.

Examples:

- King: rightful authority → pride or domination
- Queen: social embodiment of value → status dependence or court intrigue
- Paladin: principled protection → moral rigidity or crusading control
- Great Mother: containment and care → engulfment or infantilization
- Fool: exploratory courage → scattered novelty seeking
- Trickster: adaptive disruption → manipulation or corrosive irony
- Steward: preservation → excessive caution or dead administration
- Alchemist: transformative correspondence → obscurity, mystification, or endless transmutation

The useful question is not whether an archetype is good or bad, but whether it remains proportionate, reality-responsive, and integrated with the whole.

## Correction through neglected functions

Each mask requires counterweights.

A correction is not necessarily the opposite archetype. It is the priority or function excluded by the captured configuration.

Examples:

- A captured King may require Harmony, humility before reality, or capacity to receive correction.
- A captured Great Mother may require Dignity, boundaries, and permission for separation.
- A captured Fool may require Security, Comfort, completion, and conservation.
- A captured Steward may require Acquisition, experimentation, and tolerance for uncertainty.

This makes the system developmental rather than merely typological.

## Memory structure

The system can be recalled as a three-by-three matrix.

Rows:

> **Dignity — Security — Acquisition**

Columns:

> **Recognition — Harmony — Comfort**

Each cell contains two possible directions of governance.

The system therefore does not require memorizing eighteen disconnected types. Remember the nine structural pairings, then reverse governance.
